Wenjing Yang Program

Intestinal immune microenvironment and CD4⁺ T cell functional remodeling

Dr. Yang studies how intestinal microenvironmental signals shape CD4⁺ T cell fate and function in inflammatory bowel disease. Her work integrates mucosal immunology, T cell biology, metabolic regulation, mouse colitis models, and translational datasets to understand inflammation, repair, and long-term intestinal outcomes.

Wenjing Yang

Selected research areas

These paragraphs are written in formal academic style and can be further customized for faculty applications, lab websites, or professional profiles.

Microenvironmental control of T cell function

Understanding how microbiota-derived metabolites, nutrient-related pathways, and host sensing mechanisms influence pathogenic and regulatory CD4⁺ T cell programs during intestinal inflammation.

Regulatory T cells and IL-10 biology

Defining mechanisms that enhance IL-10–producing Treg differentiation, stability, and suppressive function in colitis, with attention to metabolic and epigenetic regulation.

Mucosal repair and intestinal fibrosis

Investigating how CD4⁺ T cell-derived signals contribute to epithelial repair, chronic inflammation, and fibrotic complications in inflammatory bowel disease.
